Construction Youth Trust

Tommy Farrell, Head of Development

‘Our strategic partnership with the Monday Charitable Trust has been transformational. With their support, we have been able to expand our programmes, helping disadvantaged and under-represented young people progress successfully towards rewarding careers that match their individual skills and abilities.

Since 2021, we have delivered intensive programmes in 40+ schools and colleges across nine London boroughs, reaching nearly 2000 young people. Employer engagement has been a cornerstone of these programmes, with hundreds of volunteers contributing thousands of hours to inspire young people through career networking events, site visits, work experience placements, and employability masterclasses. So far, more than 70% of the young people who completed these programmes have progressed into meaningful employment or further education.

None of this would have been possible without the significant strategic support from the Monday Charitable Trust. Their long-term funding has given us the confidence and security to strengthen and expand our employment programmes, while also helping us build a diverse coalition of new funders and employer partners. We are incredibly grateful to the Monday Charitable Trust for their ongoing commitment, and we look forward to continuing our work to transform lives across London and North Kent with their support.’

The King’s Trust

Jonathan Townsend, Chief Executive Officer

‘We are delighted to partner with The Monday Charitable Trust. Like The King’s Trust they are committed to tackling disadvantage and supporting marginalised groups to flourish and realise their potential. The surety of funding they have provided has helped to underpin our work with young people often considered ‘the hardest to reach’, furthest from the job market and most at risk of disengaging in society. Together, through our Explore Programme, we are supporting these young people to develop confidence, motivation and other key skills that will underpin their future success.

The Explore programme uses a range of engaging opportunities such as first aid training, outdoor activities, cooking and other life skill sessions to positively engage with these young people, develop their skills and build relationships with our specialist staff. Our staff and young people then work together to find and secure a positive next step, whether that be education, training or employment. Support of The Monday Charitable Trust has played a key role in delivering this work and helping us to transform the life chances of hundreds of young people in recent years. We are very grateful of their support to date and look forward to working together moving forward.’

IntoUniversity

Millie Radley, Trusts and Foundations Manager

‘The Monday Charitable Trust provides invaluable support towards IntoUniversity’s work in coastal locations across the UK, where many young people and families face a number of specific challenges. A variety of factors such as limited transport links, geographic isolation, low wages in a seasonally dependent economy, as well as unaffordable and low quality housing, affect these areas specifically, and can create significant barriers to young people’s chances of success.

The Trust’s most generous backing has enabled us to provide essential academic, pastoral and skills-development support at our learning centres in Brighton, Clacton-on-Sea, Great Yarmouth, Hull, Southampton and Weston-super-Mare. Furthermore, it has also supported us to launch two transformational new learning centres in Bridlington and Grimsby, which will reach thousands of new young people in need, equipping them with the guidance, knowledge and skills to achieve their full potential.

Client Earth

Laura Clarke, CEO

‘In this critical decade for our planet, the Monday Charitable Trust is helping us deliver a future where people and nature thrive together. With their support, we're using the law to hold the corporate world accountable for its impact on climate, biodiversity and pollution. And together we're making financial systems work in service of protecting, not harming, the planet. We are truly grateful to have friends like the Trust who share our passion for preserving our environment. Thanks to them, our team of legal experts can continue to drive the change our planet so urgently needs.’

Royal College of Art

Professor Christoph Lindner, President and Vice-Chancellor

‘The Royal College of Art’s new Battersea Campus was formally opened in May 2022. The Monday Charity Library and Lane Library Terrace are key spaces within the new Campus, forming the heart of the collaborative and social spaces of this new exciting building for the College. We are truly grateful for the Monday Charitable Trust’s support and engagement with the College’s mission to deliver transformational experiences for our students and ensure the UK continues to lead the world in art and design, particularly given the long family history attached to this site in Battersea.’
